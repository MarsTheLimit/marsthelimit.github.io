Brainwave Frequencies



Delta Waves (.1 Hz - 3.5 Hz)

These are the lowest frequencies your brain operates at, and occur in mostly deep sleep. The higher the amount of delta waves in your brain, the less you are aware of in the surrounding world. While producing delta waves, your brain is able to access memories in your unconscious mind, memories you would be unable to access while in your everyday life. Keep in mind, you will not be able to control what or when this happens. Some of these memories would be childhood experiences and knowing how to ride a bike. This relaxation is the deepest out of all the waves, but the cost is your consciousness and awareness of the physical world. While relaxing with these waves, people can even relieve pain and heal wounds. Essentially, you can apply the benefits of sleeping to listening to these waves, such as immune system support. It is best to listen to these waves in a very relaxed and distraction free environment to get the effects fastest. Listening to these waves can help people to fall asleep faster, and it can improve the quality of sleep. An interesting note is that delta waves can be used to deepen hypnosis or even allow for spiritual experiences. In short, delta waves are for the deepest relaxation, healing, and unconscious processes.

Theta Waves (4 Hz - 8 Hz)

The Theta Frequency range is strong while meditating, praying, internal focus, and spiritual awareness. This frequency is often present in people who are drowsy, as it is classified as slower brain activity. It correlates to types of breakthrough creativity (such as an “eureka!” moment), intuition, and daydreaming. These categories all have something in common; they are a mix of reality and mere thoughts. While operating on this frequency, your brain also moves information from short term memory to long term. It can be useful to learn new languages with this, as the information you process is transferred from short term to long term quicker than normal, allowing you to retain more information effectively. This can also be called subconscious learning. Because this frequency range is present in light sleep, listening to this frequency can help you to deeply relax, and even be hypnotized. People are most suggestible when operating on theta waves, hence the deep relaxation. Additionally, due to the drowsiness of this state, it is useful for visualizing and coming up with new ideas such as the “eureka!” moments. These waves, like the delta waves, can help you fall asleep faster, but they most likely won’t improve sleep quality as they are for lighter sleep than delta waves. To sum it all up, theta waves are the best for subconscious learning, deep relaxation, aha moments, and meditation.

Alpha Waves (8 Hz - 13 Hz)

Alpha waves are similar to Theta waves, but are used for more active learning and meditation. These waves still relax you, but not as deeply as the previous sections. Alpha waves are useful in meditation; they can bring you to a peaceful state, but they won’t make you feel drowsy. You are still able to think in this state, and it can even heighten your creativity. However, this creativity is a more thoughtful one, not an out of the blue subconscious breakthrough. A term to describe this would be abstract thinking. Alpha waves are most common in people who are at peace. They are alert, but not processing information, making it almost like a standby state; it is easy to shift from working on one thing to another. This state allows for you to be productive and to swiftly accomplish tasks. You can increase alpha waves on your own by closing your eyes and taking deep breaths. It can act as a reset button. Alpha is the most important in learning and applying information. Not to confuse with theta, which is learning things that are facts. Facts can be memorized, but it takes a higher level of consciousness to apply facts or learn more complex topics. Interestingly, introverted people tend to show less alpha waves compared to extroverts. When alpha is present, people tend to be in good moods, view the world how it is (as opposed to a self-perceived version people who are angry or sad tend to have), and have a sense of calmness. There are two sections of alpha waves:

  • 8 - 10 Hz: inner awareness, balance, mind/body integration
  • 10 - 12 Hz: centering, healing, mind/body connection

In summary, alpha waves are for creative thinking and problem solving, resetting yourself to allow you to move on to a new task, meditation, and learning and applying information.

Beta Waves (13 Hz - 30 Hz)

Common when people have their eyes open and are listening and thinking during analytical problem solving, judgment, decision making, processing information about the surrounding environment. They are prevalent when you are solving a complex math problem, intellectual thinking, conversations, or if you’re working under pressure. It is dominant in more logical thinking, like the progression of aha creativity, to problem solving creativity, and now to analytical problem solving. They also help with focus and productivity, which can help with studying by keeping your concentration in check. However, too high of a presence of beta waves can cause anxiety, tension, and a mental burnout. They can affect one’s ability to sleep if present before someone tries to sleep. There are three types of beta waves:

  • 13 - 15 Hz: A relaxed, but focus state. You are relaxed enough to focus, but not as relaxed as lower frequency waves mentioned before.
  • 15 - 18 Hz: Aware of self and surroundings. They can enhance your conscious attention, perception, and active thinking. In other words, you are more aware of details that surround you and make you more focused and engaged in the world around you.
  • 18 - 30 Hz: At this stage of Beta waves, you are very alert of everything going on around you. It can also increase your agitation in addition to this alertness. An example of this would be you just got brake checked while driving on a busy road, so you jump awake and slam on the brakes. After this event you are hyper aware and scanning for obstacles that might be harmful.

Beta waves are important in situations that need high alert attention. Up until this point, the other frequencies do not put you into an alert enough state to make you able to effectively respond in conversations or situations that require an active thinking mindset. In short, beta waves apply when you need focus, or you need to be more alert. You could try to use this to wake yourself up in the morning and get your brain working. But just a reminder, too much of these waves could make you needlessly stressed out. If you ever do feel stressed, move down to a less intense frequency such as theta.

Gamma Waves (>30 Hz)

The highest of all the waves, these waves, if we follow the trend of the previous categories, make you highly alert and capable of solving complex problems. These waves are associated with high level problem solving. A deficiency in these waves can contribute to a learning disorder. Playing this in your ears can contribute to activating one’s brain if they are feeling drowsy, but like beta waves, they can create stress and mental exhaustion. Although they are the complete opposite of theta waves, these gamma waves are able to cause aha moments in the complete opposite way. These creative aha moments can either be obtained from the subconscious or from deeper and highly intelligent thinking, such as the thinking induced from gamma waves. Due to the higher brain speed, the gamma waves can increase one’s awareness and consciousness, and can help process information faster and more efficiently. This is partly due to the fact that gamma waves connect different brain regions to each other. The regions get a chance to work together which improves complex thinking and decision making. Gamma waves are very helpful in many areas, but too many gamma waves causes overstimulation, restlessness, and anxiety. If you feel this happening to you, it is recommended that you switch to a less intense frequency such as theta. Gamma is crucial in problem solving, critical thinking, and high level learning. Too much can cause overstimulation and anxiety, so it is important to take care while using this frequency.
