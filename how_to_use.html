How to Use

Our brains are very complex and wondrous organs of the human body. They are like super computers; processing millions of data every second, our brains are vastly more advanced than any computer that we could build today. This website’s goal is to teach people how to harness their brain’s capabilities with merely sound. Playing a frequency in one ear and a difference in the other activates the brain’s frequency-following response (FFR) and eventually syncs up. When you subtract the larger frequency from the smaller, that is the frequency your brain will eventually sync up to.

Called binaural beats, you can use them to alter the speed at which your brain is processing information. To use these binaural beats, you must first figure out what state you are trying to achieve. For example, if you want to memorize facts, you would play theta waves while studying the facts before bed. If you wanted to work on complex math problems but you’re having trouble focusing, you would want to play low-end beta waves (13 Hz). To learn more about these beats and which frequency range is best for what, go to this link. To play the frequencies, you need to decide on the base tone. Make sure not to play too high of a tone, or you could damage your eardrums. The tone doesn't really matter, it's just personal preference.

Once you have chosen your beat type, you want to get headphones. These beats work by playing one frequency in the left ear, and a different frequency in the right ear. To achieve this, you need stereo headphones. AirPods are an example of this. If you want to figure out if your headphones will work, search “headphone stereo check” in your browser. Along with stereo headphones, noise-canceling ones will help, but are not necessary. This is due to these frequencies working more efficiently when you are not distracted by other things. For better effects, you should find a quiet spot, and get into a comfortable position. If you are using lower frequencies such as Delta or Theta, it could help to dim the lights or close your eyes.

Along with these considerations, there are different ways to listen to the frequencies. For more intense waves, such as Beta and Gamma (used for focusing, alertness, and studying), it could be useful to play them at quieter volumes while working or studying. This is called passive listening, and is best used with lower base tones. For sleep, relaxation, or even hypnosis, you could try active listening. This works best with Delta and Theta waves on slightly higher base tones. To actively listen, you should focus entirely on the sound and breathe deeply to enhance your relaxation. Theta can be used to study, however, so it is suggested that for studying, or other activities that require concentration, you listen to the sounds passively. Beta and Gamma will mostly be used passively while Delta primarily actively. For Theta and Alpha, it depends on what you are trying to accomplish.

Lastly, if you are trying to meditate, or even before you dive into learning, a helpful brain tip is to visualize what you are trying to accomplish. For example, if you want to come up with a great idea while listening to Theta, picture yourself coming up with a great idea while actively listening. There are many ways to interact with the frequencies while listening, so experiment to see what works best for you.

You should also take into consideration how long you want your session to be. For general guidelines:

  • Short session (10-15 minutes): Good for focus, quick relaxation, or boosting energy. For example, Alpha for relaxing and Beta for focusing.
  • Medium session (20-40 minutes): Best for meditation, creativity, or problem-solving. Best used with Theta and Alpha waves.
  • Long session (45+ minutes): Best for deep sleep (Delta), hypnosis, or deep relaxation (Theta).

If followed, these considerations will dramatically increase the effectiveness of the binaural beats.
